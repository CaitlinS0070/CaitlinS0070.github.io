04
ArcGIS Pro Choropleth Economic Mobility

Educational Attainment by State (2023)

A choropleth map displaying the share of residents aged 25 and older holding a Bachelor's degree or higher across all U.S. states and territories. Classified into ten ranges from 23.3% to 63.6%, the map reveals strong regional clustering in degree attainment, with Washington D.C. shown as a separate inset at the highest range nationally.

05
ArcGIS Pro Choropleth Economic Mobility

Federal Student Loan Debt by State (2024)

A choropleth map of the 2024 average federal student loan balance outstanding per borrower by state, ranging from roughly $30,000 to over $55,000. Washington D.C. borrowers carry the highest average burden of any state or territory. Supports analysis of how student debt geography intersects with educational attainment and economic opportunity.

06
ArcGIS Pro Choropleth Health & Equity

Poverty Among Degree Holders by State (2023)

A choropleth map of the share of U.S. adults aged 25 and older with a Bachelor's degree who are living below the poverty line, by state. Rates range from under 3.1% to over 16%, with significant regional variation. The map surfaces geographic disparities in economic outcomes for degree holders, providing relevant context for workforce development and social services policy.

07
R Choropleth Graduate Capstone Economic Mobility

Average Milestone Achievement by State (2015-2024)

Created in R using IPUMS U.S. Census data as part of a graduate capstone analyzing adult milestone achievement, skilled trades, and the higher education system. The map displays state-level averages of a composite milestone index (0-4) for individuals aged 25 to 45, covering 2015 to 2024. Regional variation in milestone attainment informs the capstone's core policy argument for reforming Career and Technical Education pathways in U.S. high schools.
